Notable Disasters

Action Park

Frequently referred to as "Class Action Park" or "Accident Park" by visitors, Action Park had an extremely poor safety record, resulting in at least six deaths due to ride mishaps. Located in Vernon Township, NJ, it is widely considered the worst theme park of all time.

A few particularly notable attractions stand out.

  • Alpine Slide: A plastic sled in a concrete tobaggan. Two speeds: "Snail in peanut butter" and "Death awaits."
  • Cannonball Falls: Park guests could ride down one of two dark, covered slides before landing in pool that was up to 30 degrees colder than other park pools.
  • The Diving Cliffs: Artificially constructed to appear as natural cliffs in a tropical environment. Many people weren't aware of the jumpers in the pool below, resulting in numerous injuries.
  • Surf Hill: Multi-lane hump slide common to many water parks today... except for the seventh lane, which had an extra hill. It was commonly referred to as the "back-breaker."
  • Cannonball Loop: Undoubtedly the worst attraction ever created - an enclosed water slide with a full vertical loop in the middle. Seriously. This was real.

Six Flags New Orleans

The park opened in 2000 under the name "Jazzland," and was acquired by Six Flags in 2003 and renamed Six Flags New Orleans. In 2005, hurricane Katrina hits and floods the entire park. As a result, it has since been abandoned. The gates remain locked. Several attempts were made to reopen the park under various owners, but none were successful. Several Blockbuster movies have taken advantage of the creepy atmosphere.

Splendid China Florida

Opened in 1993, this park had over 60 replica landmarks at 1/10th scale. It cost about $100 million to make, although there were no rides. Unfortunately for the park, it drew criticism for its ownership by the Chinese government. Additionally, a number of park performers sought political asylum in the U.S., which was a huge embarassment to park management. The park was torn down in 2013.
