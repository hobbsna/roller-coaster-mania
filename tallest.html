The 10 Tallest Coasters Ever Made

10. Hyperion - 269 foot drop at 84 degrees. From the polish park Enerylandia.

9. Intimidator 305 - 300 foot drop. Kings Dominion, Virginia. What this ride lacks in inversions it makes up for in intense g-forces and ridiculious speed.

8. Millenium Force - 300 foot drop; 310 feet tall. Accerates to 93 mph and lets rider experience a "grayout," but doesn't have much airtime.

7. Leviathan - 306 foot drop. The tallest coaster in Canada, and incredibly smoooth as well.

6. Steel Dragon 2000 - 307 foot drop. A Japanese behemoth located in Nagashima Spa Land, this breathtaking four minute ride will be sure to impress all who visit it.

5. Fury 325 - 320 foot drop. Widely acknowledged as one of the best coasters in the world, this ride in Carowinds, North Carolina boasts an 81 deggree drop and a top speed of 95 mph!

4. Superman: Escape from Krypton - 328 foot drop. The first to drop more than 300 feet with a tower over 400 feet. Theoretical top speed of 100 mph.

3. Red Force - 345 foot drop. Top-hat tower from Spain that goes straight up and comes straight back down. Uses magnetic motors for propulsion and is built on a Ferrari theme. Reaches 112 mph in 5 seconds!

2. Top Thrill Dragster - 400 Foot Drop, Cedar Point, Ohio. Only 17 seconds long, but what a ride! This is the one that started the coaster war.

1. Kingda Ka - A stupid 456 feet up in the air. 50.6 seconds of pure adrenaline, with speeds up 128 mph. For now, Six Flags, New Jersey is king of the tallest roller coasters.
