Amusement Park History

Europe introduces "Pleasure Gardens," which include flower gardens, fountains, bowling, music, dancing, bowling, and a few primitive amusement rides.

Ice slides, supported with heavy timbers, become a popular activity in Russia.

Pleasure garden exported to America - Vauxhall gardens opened.

Coney Island opens, linked to New York City via roadway.

First looping gravity railway at Frascati Gardens, Paris.

Coney Island starts becoming a popular seaside resort thanks to the completition of the first railroad to Coney Island, Brooklyn, NY.

Switchback Gravity Pleasure Railway at Coney Island. The first true roller coaster.

First electric powered street railway - Richmond, VA. "Trolley parks spread rapidly

Lina Beecher creates the first vertical looping coaster in Toledo, which is later relocatd to Coney Island.

Chutes Park opens in Chicago. First park to be enclosed and charged an admission.

More then 2000 Amusement parks in operation in the U.S.

John Miller Patents his design for the underfriction roller coaster. This new method reduces drag, allowing for higher and steeper drops, along with faster speeds. Completely changes the amusement park industry.

Golden age of amusment parks. "The Great Wild Ride building boom" ensues.

Stock market crash + Great depresssion = many closed parks.

Amusment parks are diversion form War, but rationing and scarcity mean slowed growth.

Baby boomer era results in Kiddielands, but rapidly rising propert values result in short-lived phenomenon.

Disneyland opens. Widely considerd the first theme park.

Matterhown premiers at Disneyland - first major tubular steel coaster. Completely changes roller coaster development.

First Six Flags opens in Texas.

Log Flumes introduced at Six Flags, Texas.

Large Inner City parks begin closing due to turmoil.

Walt Disney World opens in Florida.

Kings Island opens and revivs classic wooden roller coaster.

EPCOT Center opens at Walt Disney World, Florida.

Disneyland, Toyko opens.

Several publicly owned companies purchase other amusement parks to create large multi-unit operators.

Batman, the Ride opens at Six Flags Great America in Illinois. First inverted roller coaster (cars travel underneath structure)

Roller coaster arms race reaches its peak. Coaster developers keep trying to make coasters than are taller and faster than anything else in the world.

Kingda Ka at Six Flags adventure stops arms race - growing costs and narrow appeal for new rides.

New wave of park closings driven by increasing real estate values and financial problems.

Markets continue to emerge globally, technology advances, and immersive thrills become the thing to do (ex. Wizarding World of Harry Potter at Univesal Islands of Adventure in Florida and Galaxy's Edge at Hollywood studios.)
