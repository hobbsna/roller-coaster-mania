The 10 Fastest Coasters Ever Made

10. Leviathan - 92 mph, and very smooth for it's extreme speed. Fastest coaster in Canada.

9. Millenium Force - 93 mph of sheer dizzyness. No out-of-seat airtime after first drop. Located in Cedar Point, Ohio.

8. Fury 325 - 95 mph (tied with 7). Among Carowinds, North Carolina's most well-known rides, Fury 325 begins with a traditional lift for an enormous hill and then you'd better hold on tight.

7. Steel Dragon 2000 - 95 mpgh (tied with number 8). From Nagashima Spa Land, Japan, this ride features a traditional lift to rist to the top, followed by an out-and-back adventure.

6. Superman: Escape From Krypton- a perfect 100 mph. It has new cars now, so it could theoretically even be slightly faster. The coaster is located in Six Flags Magic Mountain, California.

5. Dodonpa - 112 mph (tied with number 4). From Fuji-Q Highland, Japan, this coaster uses a compressed air launch, going from 0 to 112 in just under two seconds. The entire ride lasts only 55 seconds!

4. Red Force - 112 mph (tied with number 5). Uses magnetic motors to launch its trains. Fastest and tallest roller coaster in all of Europe.

3. Top Thrill Dragster - 120 mph. Reaches nerve rattling speeds with impressive hydraulic launch system to catapult riders through the ride. Located in Cedar Point, Ohio, it is similar to Kingda Ka, but slightly slower, shorter, and smoother.

2. Kingda Ka - 128 mph, the fastest coaster in the United States, courtesy of Six Flags, New Jersey. Much like Top Thrill Dragster, this bad boy uses a hydraulic launch system.

1. Formula Rossa - 149.1 mph, and it only takes 4.9 seconds to get there via hydraulic launch. The speed is so intense that passengers are issues goggles to protect their eyes from the desert sand in Abu Dhabi (belongs to the United Arab Emirates)!
